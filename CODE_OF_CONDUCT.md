# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:
- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences — especially relevant here, since this
  project spans hardware, firmware, ML, and web development, and no single contributor is expected to
  be expert in all of them.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism, particularly on the safety-critical firmware logic.
- Focusing on what is best for the project and, ultimately, for the people this system is meant to help
  get out of an ambulance's way faster.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:
- Harassment, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a GitHub issue marked "conduct" or
contacting the maintainer directly. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated.
